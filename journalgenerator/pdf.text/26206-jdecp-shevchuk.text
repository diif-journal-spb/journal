Reaction di usion model of post-infarction myocardial remodeling
Abstract. W e propose a reaction di usion model for post-infarction myocardial
remodeling with  ve tissue states: healthy myocardium, in ammatory in ltrate,
necrosis, replacement  brosis, and interstitial  brosis. The state v ariables are
the concentration parameters of a Dirichlet distribution, which makes it possible
to describe both the mean tissue composition and the uncertainty in transition
zones. F or the resulting quasilinear parabolic system with anisotropic di usion,
we establish uniform ellipticity of the di usion tensor, local Lipschitz continuity of
the reaction operator, global existence of nonnegative weak solutions, inv ariance
of the admissible region, an exponential lower bound, and uniqueness under
additional regularity . F or an auxiliary reversible transition matrix, we derive an
entropy balance, while for the irreversible matrix we obtain a spectral stability
condition for the linearized system. F or the Lie splitting scheme, we state positivity
conditions and an error estimate in the smooth regime. Numerical examples
reproduce the standard phase ordering of post-infarction remodeling and provide
a basis for a subsequent inverse problem driven by LGE-MRI data.
Keywords: quasilinear parabolic system; Dirichlet concentration parameters;
myocardial  brosis; global existence; uniqueness under additional regularity;
entropy balance; anisotropic di usion; inverse problem.
